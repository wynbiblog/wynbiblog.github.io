Don’t let your Resume Ruin your Chances

Avoid Typos and Grammatical Errors The smallest mistake can make you look sloppy. Note outcomes Instead of Duties Don't just list off task you performed, show your outcomes. Nix the Irrelevant Work Experience Get rid of work experience that is irrelevant. That job serving snow cones in high school won't help you get the marketing position you are applying for now. Tailor Your Resume to the Position Employers want to see a resume that is tailored to the specific position you are applying for. Use Action Verbs Switch out overused words with action verbs—below is a great resource. https://www.themuse.com/advice/185-powerful-verbs-that-will-make-your-resume-awesome Too Creative Don't let fonts and style distract from important experience. Ditch Company Specific Jargon Use terms that are the norm in a specific industry, not words or systems that are unique to your current company.
